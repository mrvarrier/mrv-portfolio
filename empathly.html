Empath.ly

A video conferencing platform with integrated emotional analytics, helping HR and marketing teams track emotional dynamics during remote meetings.

Role
UX Designer & Researcher
Team
2 Developers, 1 Business Analyst
Duration
36 Hours
Awards
1st Place Overall, Best Life Hack

Overview

Empath.ly is an innovative video conferencing platform with integrated emotional analytics and real-time emotion identification. During video calls, our system analyzes participants' emotions sixty times per second through their camera feed. After each call, users can access a comprehensive dashboard displaying emotional data and actionable insights, including predominant emotions throughout the meeting, emotional patterns over time, and notable shifts or contrasts in participants' emotional states.

Context

Remote work has created an emotional disconnect in how we communicate through screens.

COVID-19 changed how we communicate and work by making remote interactions normal. However, this shift created a major emotional gap - humans weren't designed to connect emotionally through screens.

Research Process

Our research process involved three key approaches to understand both the problem and potential solution space:

Desk Research

Our business analyst and I investigated why people prefer remote jobs and why certain video platforms succeed while others fail. This helped us identify opportunities for our solution.

User Interviews

I spoke with regular users of Google Meet and Zoom to understand their experiences. These conversations during the hackathon gave us valuable insights into user expectations and pain points.

Key Insights

INSIGHT #1

Users preferred Zoom's richer feature set but Google Meet's simpler flow. They appreciated how Google Meet let them quickly join and use the platform without cluttered interfaces.

INSIGHT #2

Through interviews with professionals across various roles, we identified our primary target audience: HR professionals and marketing teams who showed the strongest need for emotional analytics.

INSIGHT #3

Our breakthrough came when we pinpointed the core problem: hiring managers and marketing teams struggle to read emotional cues during video conferences, creating a significant emotional disconnect.

Problem Statement

"How might we help marketing and HR professionals better understand and respond to the emotional dynamics of their virtual meetings?"

Design Process

Designing for Usability

My first priority was creating a user-friendly landing page. Our research showed that users struggle to accomplish simple tasks on platforms like Zoom because of cluttered interfaces. I designed our landing page with clear pathways to start or join meetings without distractions.

Leveraging Familiar Patterns

Since users are already accustomed to platforms like Google Meet and Zoom, I recognized the importance of working with these established mental models. I designed our video calling interface with familiar elements inspired by Google Meet to reduce the learning curve.

The Data Visualization Challenge

The most significant design challenge was presenting emotional data in a meaningful and accessible way. We needed visualizations that anyone could understand at a glance, regardless of their data literacy. My team and I collaborated to explore various data representation methods.

The Solution

Respecting Time Constraints

Many users have back-to-back meetings and don't always have time to view detailed breakdowns. I designed a pie chart dashboard that pops up once the meeting ends, helping users quickly understand the emotions felt throughout the meeting before hopping to their next call.

Comprehensive Analytics

The dashboard was designed for maximum scannability. It includes the quick overview pie chart, a timeline graph showing emotional flow throughout the meeting, and highlighted transcription excerpts where specific emotions spiked. These three different presentation methods meet various user needs.

Results & Impact

Project Recognition

Our project earned significant recognition, winning first place overall and the "Best Life Hack" award at HackHarvard. Additionally, Empath.ly was selected as one of the top 80 startups among more than 1,500 competitors in India.

Personal Growth

This intensive hackathon experience greatly advanced my skills as a UX designer. I gained valuable expertise in data visualization techniques and deepened my understanding of how different user contexts impact design decisions.

Future Development

Moving from Standalone to Integration

Our key insight for future development is that creating a standalone application isn't optimal. Users already have established workflows with platforms like Zoom and Google Meet. Our next step is to develop Empath.ly as a plugin for existing platforms, delivering emotional analytics within the tools users already rely on daily.
